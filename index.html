Dreamwell

Choose what you dream. Rest inside what you want.

The Dreamwell apple allows users to select and experience specific dreams on demand. Designed for rest, imagination, replay, experimentation, and selected forms of escape.

The story of Dreamwell begins with apples.

Long before it became a device, the inventor had a private theory: apples were never just fruit. They carried people out of ordinary life. One bite led to exile, another to enchanted sleep, another to immortality, another to revelation. Apples did not merely feed people. They interrupted reality.

She began to suspect they belonged not to the kitchen, but to the threshold between waking and dreaming.

At first, it was only an obsession. She filled notebooks with sketches, copied fragments from myths, and collected strange references to apples wherever she found them. Later, while reading sleep reports and dream journals, she noticed a similar pattern: people rarely described dreams as something they created. They described them as something they slipped into through a small shift, an ordinary detail, a surreal turn they only recognized too late. That was what the apple had always done in stories. It was never the destination itself, only the beautiful, familiar object that carried someone across.

That was the moment the apple stopped being just a symbol.

She had been looking for a way into dreams—something that could hold their threshold without breaking it. But the forms she first experimented with felt wrong. They were cold and futuristic, too metallic and too medical, too eager to explain themselves. They looked like machines for measuring sleep, not entering it. Gradually, another form began to clarify itself: an apple, something round, seductive, familiar, almost innocent.

That is how Dreamwell took its final shape. It looked like something ancient and mythical. The thread-like connector was intentional too: part umbilical cord, part fate line, part fairy-tale path through the woods.

Some people say Dreamwell was invented.

But we know it was simply waiting inside the apple all along.

Manifesto

Everyone deserves a chance to dream.

We no longer accept that stress, anxiety, poor mental health, and the limitations of daily life should take away a person’s ability to rest, imagine, and experience what they long for.

We believe sleep can hold comfort without becoming denial. We believe desire deserves a room, even when waking life has locked the door. We believe that people prevented by social, financial, physical, or emotional constraints from living certain hopes in reality should still be given a space to safely experience possibility.

Dreamwell is not a promise to replace life. It is a question about what life withholds, what dreams repair, and what happens when the private theater of sleep becomes designed.

The Apple

Dreamwell is a wearable sleep device that allows users to select and experience designed dream scenarios.

Paired with the Dreamwell app, it supports pre-designed dream environments, custom dream creation, and shared dream interaction.

Usage Steps

  1. Open the Dreamwell app and select a dream.
  2. Choose a preset, customize your own scenario, or start Linkage Mode.
  3. Tie the thinner line around your phone.
  4. Secure the thicker loop around your wrist.
  5. Place the apple near you and set the phone beside the bed.
  6. Close your eyes and fall asleep. Dreamwell will carry you into the selected dream environment.

Release Info

Dreamwell is scheduled for release in 2049. Early access will be distributed as an archive object: one apple device, one wearable thread loop, one return journal, and one sealed dream catalog.

Subscription Plan

After the initial release, users can purchase seasonal dream catalogs to access new pre-designed scenarios and updates to the dream library. A subscription also includes access to a digital platform for sharing custom scenarios, linking dreams with other users, and receiving personalized dream recommendations.

Warnings

Please read the following notes and risk factors carefully before using the device.

  • Repeated use may increase emotional attachment to selected dream scenarios, recurring environments, or idealized versions of people and places.
  • Some users report preferring designed dream experiences over waking reality during periods of stress, loneliness, grief, or social withdrawal.
  • Dreamwell should not be used to postpone decisions, conversations, deadlines, or responsibilities that require waking attention.
  • The wearable loop may cause discomfort during sleep in some positions or with extended overnight use.
  • The thread connector may tangle with bedding, hair, jewelry, charging cables, or nearby objects if not placed carefully before sleep.
  • Do not wrap, knot, or tighten the thread around the wrist, neck, fingers, or bed frame.
  • Some users experience waking disorientation, dream residue, or temporary confusion between dream memory and waking memory.
  • Dream residue may appear as smell, texture, guilt, confidence, dread, hunger, or the feeling that someone has just left the room.
  • Custom scenarios may become more emotionally intense than expected once sleep begins.
  • A selected dream may shift tone, plot, or emotional outcome without notice.
  • Dreamwell cannot guarantee that the unconscious will obey the selected genre, mood, or ending.
  • Linkage Mode may produce mismatched recall, emotional asymmetry, accidental disclosure, or disagreements about what occurred inside the dream.
  • Shared use may reveal emotional information neither user intended to disclose.
  • Repeated use may alter the user’s relationship to boredom, waiting, longing, grief, and ordinary sleep.
  • Designed dreams may interrupt the natural processing function of sleep by replacing difficulty with satisfaction too quickly.
  • Recommended use is occasional rather than nightly.
  • Leave at least one unstructured dream night between custom or high-intensity scenarios.
  • If the same unselected dream figure appears repeatedly, remove the object from the room for one full night before reuse.
  • If reality feels unusually flat, delayed, or unconvincing after waking, discontinue use and reorient before re-entry.
  • Waking disappointment is not considered a product malfunction.
